From The Power Paradox to Echo


Purpose

The concept of our project has been meticulously developed to illuminate the pervasive state of misinformation and manipulation that impacts our information consumption, polarizing views through the influence of AI and political agendas. In the realm of net culture, digital citizens are increasingly trapped within echo chambers, where the repeated exposure to similar ideas not only amplifies misinformation but also crafts a distorted reality. This perpetuates a cycle where the public is constantly misled by fragmented yet compelling narratives driven by corporations, the power of AI, and government agendas. Our installation aims to encapsulate the contemporary state of our society through a multimedia and multi-sensory interactive experience, creating beautified, illusional spaces that engage the public who may not realize their true surroundings or the nature of the information they consume. This project seeks to provoke thoughtful discussion and challenge these pervasive issues. We intend to engage audiences directly on these critical matters and explore the dynamics of digital citizenship in our interconnected world.

This is a collaborative project involving Binna Lee as the Creative Director, Sichun Zhang as the Visual Designer, Roy Yang as the Creative Technician, and Jay Torin as the Sound Designer. Together, we aim to highlight the transformative potential of artistic expression to challenge prevailing narratives and reevaluate the impact of our digital engagements.



This installation uses visual and audio-reactive technologies to create an interactive, projection-mapped hollow cube sculpture with threads. The cube is filled with fragments of cascading lights that dynamically respond to audience voices and soundscapes, symbolizing the echo chambers of digital media.
